How this is calculated

  1. Effective rate = base rate + markup (₹ per unit of currency). This is the rate actually applied, versus the interbank/mid-market rate you enter — confirmed against an actual FX-Retail trade: a trade price of ₹96.30/USD plus a ₹0.20 markup gave a final price of ₹96.50/USD.
  2. ACE (Amount of Currency Exchanged) = foreign currency amount × effective rate. This is the INR value of the currency being bought, and the base on which GST slabs are computed.
  3. Commission: a flat fee of ₹500 for remittances up to USD 500 (or equivalent), or ₹1,000 above that, plus 18% GST on the commission. Confirmed against an actual FX-Retail/CRED trade: a USD 500 purchase was charged a flat ₹500 commission (not a percentage of ACE), with ₹45 CGST + ₹45 SGST (18% total) on top.
  4. GST on ACE follows CGST Rule 32(2)(b), which taxes a "value of supply" slab rather than the full ACE:
    • Up to ₹1,00,000 → 1% of ACE, minimum ₹250
    • ₹1,00,001–₹10,00,000 → ₹1,000 + 0.5% of ACE above ₹1,00,000
    • Above ₹10,00,000 → ₹5,500 + 0.1% of ACE above ₹10,00,000, capped at a value of supply of ₹60,000
    18% GST is then charged on that value of supply. Confirmed against the same trade: ACE of ₹48,250 gives a value of supply of ₹482.50 (1% of ACE), and 18% of that is ₹86.85 — matching the ₹43.43 CGST + ₹43.43 SGST actually charged.

    Important: the "value of supply" (₹482.50 in this example) is not a fee you are charged — it never appears as a debit on its own. It is a notional figure that GST law uses purely as the tax base, so that GST isn't calculated on the entire ACE (which would make the tax bill roughly 100× larger). The only real debit from this slab is the GST on it (CGST + SGST). This rule is the same across all banks and forex platforms — it has nothing to do with which platform (e.g. RemitNow, FX-Retail, CRED) you transacted through.
  5. TCS (Tax Collected at Source) under LRS, applied to cumulative remittances in the financial year (post Budget 2025 rates, effective 1 Apr 2025):
    • Education funded by a specified loan: 0%, no threshold
    • Education (self-funded) / medical treatment: nil up to ₹10 lakh, 2% above (assumes a linked/operative PAN — the rate rises to 5% if your PAN is inoperative)
    • All other purposes (travel, gifts, investments, maintenance, etc.): nil up to ₹10 lakh, 20% above, regardless of PAN status
    TCS is not a bank charge — it is deposited with the government against your PAN and can be claimed as a credit / refund when filing your income tax return.
  6. Correspondent Bank charges: outward remittances are routed through correspondent/intermediary banks abroad, who may levy their own processing charge. This is separate from and in addition to RemitNow's own commission. Who pays it depends on the option chosen:
    • Self — borne by you (the remitter), charged separately after the transaction is successfully processed.
    • Beneficiary — borne by the recipient; it is deducted from the remittance amount, so they receive less than the amount sent.
    • Full Value — no correspondent bank charge is levied at all. Available only for remittances in USD, EUR, or GBP, to any destination, for all personal purposes offered under RemitNow.
    The actual charge amount for Self/Beneficiary is set by the correspondent bank, varies by route/currency, and is not known in advance — so it is not included in the totals below.

    Recommendation: whenever your remittance currency is USD, EUR, or GBP, always choose Full Value — it is strictly better than the other two options since it guarantees zero correspondent bank charges, for you or the beneficiary, at no extra cost.
  7. LRS annual limit: separate from the ₹10,00,000 TCS threshold, the Liberalised Remittance Scheme itself caps total outward remittances at USD 2,50,000 per financial year, per individual — a hard regulatory limit, not just a tax trigger. This calculator estimates your cumulative USD total using the indicative USD/INR rate and warns as you approach or exceed it.
  8. Commission slab boundary: for USD remittances just above the $500 commission slab, the commission jumps from ₹500 to ₹1,000. If your amount is flexible and only slightly over $500, staying at or under it halves the commission.

Forex rates on RemitNow/FX-Retail style platforms are typically only bookable Monday–Friday during market hours (broadly 9:15 AM–3:30 PM IST). Outside this window, treat any rate you enter here as indicative only, not a bookable live rate.

This is an independent educational estimator, not an official HDFC Bank tool. Actual charges also include correspondent/intermediary bank fees that vary by route and are not known in advance. Rates, slabs and thresholds are subject to change — always confirm current charges with your bank before remitting.
